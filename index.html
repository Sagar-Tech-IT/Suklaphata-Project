Shuklaphanta National Park: Paradise For Wildlife ObservationShuklaphanta National Park is a protected area in Nepal known for its vast grasslands and high biodiversity, including the world's largest population of swamp deer. Established in 1976, it was upgraded from a wildlife reserve to a national park in 2017 and features a mix of grasslands, forests, and riverbeds. The park is a crucial habitat for endangered species like Bengal tigers and rhinoceroses, and is a popular destination for wildlife tourism and birdwatching.
